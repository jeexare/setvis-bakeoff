# Welcome to the Study

Thank you for taking part in this research! You're about to use four different tools for visualizing set data.

For each tool, you'll look at a dataset and answer 12 questions about it using the sidebar on the right. There are no trick questions; just do your best with what you can see in the visualization.


## Interacting with the visualizations
Each of the four tools is a different way of drawing set relationships, and each supports its own set of interactions. At the start of each tool, take a few seconds to explore before answering questions: try hovering over a few elements, try scrolling, try clicking. You'll quickly find out what that particular tool responds to.

Different visualizations may support different interactions. Depending on the visualization, you may be able to:

- **Hover** over a bar, line, arc, or shape to highlight it and reveal which elements belong to it.
- **Click** an element to select or pin a highlight in place.
- **Drag** to move or reposition the view.
- **Scroll**.
- **Hover** over elements to reveal additional information.
- Press the **spacebar** to highlight an intersection.

Not every visualization supports every interaction.

## Resetting the visualization
If you accidentally move or zoom the visualization, you can always return to its original state.

Press **R** at any time to reset the visualization.

## A note on loading
The visualizations are embedded live and can take a few seconds to appear: if the panel looks blank or partially loaded right after you click Next, wait a moment before assuming something's broken.
